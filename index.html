About Me

Every dataset
has a story.

I didn't start in data. I started in quality assurance — two years of keeping standards, tracking processes, and making sure things worked the way they should.

But there was one thing that never worked: the numbers. They only mattered in seasons — when a report was due, when a budget needed defending, when someone asked a question no one could answer. Always the same chaos: scattered spreadsheets, missing records, hours lost reconstructing figures that should have been a click away. Money leaving the organisation quietly, with no one watching the door.

I watched it happen one too many times. Burnt out — not from working too hard, but from working in a system that didn't respect what its own numbers were trying to say — I went to find out what they were saying.

What I found was a passion. Data tells stories — about where an organisation is bleeding, where it's thriving, where it needs to go next. My job is to find that story, shape it into something unmistakably clear, and make it easy for people to act on it.

Because the best analysis in the world means nothing if no one understands it — or knows what to do next.

01

Customer Segmentation Analysis

Understanding who your customers really are — not who you assume them to be — changes everything about how you serve them.

Python Segmentation Behaviour Analysis
S
Situation
A retail business had 246,740 chip transactions across 71,290 customers but no way to distinguish between buyer types — decisions were based on assumption, not evidence. There was no framework for targeted marketing, inventory allocation, or pricing strategy.
T
Task
Analyse transactional data to uncover distinct customer segments, validate pricing strategy through statistical testing, and surface inventory recommendations — translating findings into quantified business value.
A
Action
Cleaned and validated the dataset in Python — removing 18,094 out-of-scope records, resolving serial date format errors, and standardising 27 brand names to achieve 99.9%+ data accuracy. Applied RFM-style segmentation and causal inference techniques including Difference-in-Differences and hypothesis testing. Conducted time-series analysis to identify seasonal patterns.
R
Result
Quantified $24.7K–$30.7K in annual value creation. Proved 10.3% price premium acceptance (p<0.05) among the target segment, enabling a data-driven pricing strategy worth $14.7K in margin protection. Discovered a 28% pre-holiday sales surge through time-series analysis, generating inventory optimisation recommendations worth $10K–$16K in incremental revenue.

Customer Churn Prediction Model

An ML pipeline that identified at-risk customers before they left — giving the business time to act.

Python Scikit-learn ML
S
Situation
The organisation was losing customers at an increasing rate with no early warning system in place. Leadership only noticed churn after it had already happened — too late to intervene.
T
Task
Build a model that could predict which customers were likely to churn within the next 30 days — early enough for the retention team to step in with targeted outreach.
A

R
